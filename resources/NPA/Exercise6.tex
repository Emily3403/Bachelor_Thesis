    \subsection{}

    In the traceroute from Denmark to CDU, the packets go through the USA, crossing two oceans before reaching Australia.
    The route from TU Berlin is different: it starts in Germany, goes through the local academic network (DFN), and then uses the European GÉANT backbone to reach Australia.
    Both routes use AARNet to reach CDU in Australia.
    The TU Berlin route also shows several hops marked with \glqq * \grqq{} (ICMP timeouts), which may mean the routers block or deprioritize ICMP, even though the packets are still forwarded correctly.
    Overall, the RTT from TU Berlin was faster.
    
    \subsection{}

    One reason for the differences is the path the packets take through different networks. The TU Berlin route uses academic networks like DFN and GÉANT, which are direct and optimized to reach Australia via AARNet. The public server route goes through commercial networks and the USA, making the path longer.
    
    Another reason is the round-trip times (RTTs). The TU Berlin route has lower RTTs because the packets travel shorter and more direct paths, while the public server route has higher RTTs due to extra hops and ocean crossings.
